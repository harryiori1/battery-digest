## 01 California Bets on Sodium-Ion Batteries for Extreme Heat Regions
California renewable energy developer Juniper Energy has partnered with Alsym Energy to deploy 500 MWh of sodium-ion battery storage projects across California. This move is significant as it marks a major investment in sodium-ion battery technology, which is seen as a promising alternative to traditional lithium-ion batteries. Sodium-ion batteries have the potential to be more cost-effective and sustainable, making them an attractive option for large-scale energy storage projects. The partnership between Juniper Energy and Alsym Energy is expected to play a crucial role in California's efforts to transition to renewable energy sources and reduce its reliance on fossil fuels. ## 02 AI-Powered EV Battery Charging System Extends Battery Life
Scientists have discovered a secret to making EV batteries live years longer with a new AI-powered battery management system. The system uses artificial intelligence to optimize battery charging and discharging, which can help to extend the lifespan of EV batteries. According to reports, the AI-powered system can squeeze thousands of extra miles out of EV batteries, making it a significant breakthrough in the field of electric vehicle technology. The development of this system has the potential to reduce the cost of EV ownership and make electric vehicles more appealing to consumers. [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/news/795781/ai-powered-ev-battery-charging-system/)

## 03 GM Energy Home System Turns GM EVs into Backup Battery Power
GM has introduced a home system that allows GM EV owners to use their vehicles as backup battery power during outages. The GM Energy Home System is designed to provide homeowners with a reliable source of backup power, using the battery of their GM EV. The system is a significant innovation in the field of energy storage and has the potential to make electric vehicles even more useful to their owners. With the GM Energy Home System, homeowners can use their EVs to power their homes during outages, reducing their reliance on traditional backup power sources such as generators.
