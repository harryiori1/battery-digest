## 01 BYD Dominates Global EV Market, Expands Rapidly
BYD, the world's largest EV maker, reported over 300,000 NEV sales in March, marking a significant rebound and accelerating overseas growth. The company also outsold Tesla in Europe for the second consecutive month and is rapidly expanding its presence in Canada with plans for 20 dealerships within a year, capitalizing on surging oil prices driving EV adoption. ## 02 Sodium-Ion and Semi-Solid-State Batteries See Major Progress
The next generation of battery technology is rapidly advancing, with Hina Battery predicting sodium-ion battery costs will match lithium rivals next year, following a recent breakthrough enabling 11-minute charging and 450 km range. Meanwhile, semi-solid-state batteries are expanding beyond passenger EVs to power light trucks and flying cars, with MG Motor set to launch the first semi-solid-state EV in Europe by year-end. [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/04/01/hina-battery-exec-sodium-battery-costs-fall-to-match-lithium/) and [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/03/25/sodium-ion-ev-battery-delivers-11-min-charging-450-km-range/) and [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/03/30/semi-solid-state-ev-batteries-powering-up-trucks-flying-cars/).

## 03 BYD Accelerates Flash Charging Network Deployment
BYD has reached a milestone by deploying its 5,000th flash charging station, part of an ambitious plan to establish 20,000 sites this year. This infrastructure expansion complements new EV models like the Song Ultra, which integrates BYD's Blade Battery and Flash Charging technology
