## 01 CALB in Spotlight as GAC Aion S Vehicles Hit by Widespread Battery Cell Defects
CALB, a leading battery manufacturer, is facing scrutiny after GAC Aion S vehicles were found to have widespread battery cell defects. The defects have raised concerns about the quality and reliability of CALB's batteries, which are used in several electric vehicle models. According to reports, the defects are related to the battery cells' internal structure, which can cause them to malfunction or even catch fire. CALB has not yet commented on the issue, but it is expected to take measures to rectify the problem and prevent similar defects in the future. The incident highlights the importance of quality control in the battery manufacturing process and the need for rigorous testing to ensure the safety and reliability of electric vehicles. ## 02 China's Battery Tax Shift to Push Automakers to Make Their Own Cells, Says CPCA's Cui
China's adjustment of lithium battery consumption tax is expected to boost vehicle companies to produce their own batteries, according to Cui Dongshu, secretary general of the China Passenger Car Association (CPCA). The tax shift is seen as a strong push for automakers to invest in battery production, which will help reduce their dependence on external suppliers and improve their competitiveness in the market. Cui believes that the policy change will benefit China's automotive industry as a whole, making it larger and stronger. The tax adjustment is part of China's efforts to promote the development of the new energy vehicle industry and reduce the country's reliance on fossil fuels. ## 03 Philippines Logistics Firm Sees Swappable Battery Scooters as Next Milestone in Last-Mile Deliveries
A Philippines logistics firm is testing swappable battery scooters for last-mile deliveries, potentially reducing costs and increasing efficiency. The company, JRS Express, has partnered with mobility startup Voltai to integrate electric motorcycles and battery-swapping technology into their commercial logistics operations. The pilot project has shown promising results, with the swappable battery scooters reducing delivery times and increasing the number of deliveries per day. The use of swappable batteries also eliminates the need for lengthy charging times, allowing the scooters to be used continuously throughout the day. The success of the project could pave the way for the widespread adoption of swappable battery technology in the logistics industry.
