## 02 China's Battery Tax Shift to Push Automakers to Make Their Own Cells, CPCA's Cui Says
China's adjustment of lithium battery consumption tax is expected to have a significant impact on the automotive industry. According to Cui Dongshu, secretary general of the China Passenger Car Association (CPCA), the tax shift will push automakers to make their own battery cells. This move is seen as a way to promote the development of China's automotive industry and reduce dependence on foreign battery manufacturers. The tax adjustment is part of a broader effort to create a more level playing field for electric vehicles and traditional gasoline-powered cars.
