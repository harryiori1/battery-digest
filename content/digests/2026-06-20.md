## 01 Solid-State Batteries Now Powering EVs in the Real World
Solid-state EV batteries are now being tested on the road in North America for the first time, promising longer range, faster charging, and lower costs. This development is a significant milestone in the evolution of electric vehicle technology, as solid-state batteries have the potential to overcome many of the limitations of traditional lithium-ion batteries. With solid-state batteries, EVs can achieve longer ranges, faster charging times, and improved safety. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/06/11/solid-state-batteries-now-powering-evs-in-real-world/), this technology is being tested on the road in North America for the first time, marking a major breakthrough in the industry.

## 02 BYD Datang EV Launched with 950km Range, Globally First
The BYD Datang EV has been launched with a range of up to 950km, making it a flagship variable in a shrinking market. The Datang EV is a premium SUV that starts at 239,900 RMB ($35,502 USD) for the base model and goes up to 269,900 RMB ($39,941 USD) for the flagship trim. According to [CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/06/19/byd-datang-comparison-reveals-accelerating-ev-progress/), the Datang EV's range is class-leading, making it an attractive option for consumers in the Chinese market.

## 03 Tesla Model Y Battery Test Reveals Significant Degradation
A real-world test of a Tesla Model Y's battery has revealed significant degradation, with the battery retaining only 88% of its capacity after just 20,000km. This test was conducted by a Tesla Model Y owner, who shared their findings on [第一电动](https://www.d1ev.com/news/qiye/303557). The results are concerning, as they suggest that the Tesla Model Y's battery may not be as durable as expected. However, it's worth noting that this is just one test, and more data is needed to confirm these findings.
