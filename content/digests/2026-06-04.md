## 01 What War On EVs? New $230 Million LFP Battery Materials Factory Proposed For Texas
A new $230 million battery manufacturing facility is proposed for Texas, aimed at scaling up the production of LFP cathodes for use in EVs among other applications. The facility, which is expected to create new jobs and stimulate local economic growth, will be focused on producing high-quality LFP cathodes with a high energy density. This development is significant for the EV industry, as LFP batteries are known for their safety, reliability, and cost-effectiveness. The new facility is expected to play a crucial role in meeting the growing demand for EVs and renewable energy storage systems. According to [CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/06/04/evs-batteries-lfp-cathode-texas-invest-energyx-wildcat-holyvolt/), the facility will be built by Invest EnergyX and Wildcat, with HolyVolt providing the necessary technology and expertise.

## 02 Another Solid-State EV Battery Maker Is Going Public to Fund Its Next Growth Stage
ProLogium, a solid-state EV battery maker, announced it will become a publicly listed company through a business merger with a special purpose acquisition company (SPAC). This move is expected to provide ProLogium with the necessary funding to accelerate its growth and expand its production capacity. Solid-state batteries are considered a game-changer for the EV industry, offering improved safety, energy density, and charging speeds. ProLogium's decision to go public is a significant development in the EV battery market, as it will enable the company to compete more effectively with established players. As reported by [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/27/another-solid-state-ev-battery-maker-going-public/), ProLogium's merger with the SPAC is expected to be completed by the end of the year.

## 03 Old Waymo Batteries Will Get A Second Life As Stationary Energy Storage
Waymo, a leading autonomous vehicle company, has struck a deal with B2U Storage Solutions to repurpose its old robotaxi batteries as stationary energy storage. This innovative approach will reduce waste and increase the lifespan of the batteries, while also providing a sustainable solution for energy storage. The partnership between Waymo and B2U Storage Solutions is a significant development in the EV industry, as it highlights the potential for battery reuse and recycling. According to [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/news/797837/waymo-ev-batteries-energy-storage-recycling/), the repurposed batteries will be used to store excess energy generated by renewable sources, such as solar and wind power, and will help to stabilize the grid.
