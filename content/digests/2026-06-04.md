## 01 ProLogium to Go Public Through Merger to Fund Solid-State Battery Growth
ProLogium, a leading solid-state battery manufacturer, has announced that it will become a publicly listed company through a business merger with a special purpose acquisition company (SPAC). This move is expected to provide the necessary funding for ProLogium's next growth stage, as it continues to commercialize its solid-state battery technology. ProLogium was the first company to commercialize solid-state batteries, and this merger is seen as a significant step forward for the company. The merger is expected to provide ProLogium with the necessary resources to expand its production capacity and further develop its technology. ## 02 SAIC Launches Electric SUV with Semi-Solid-State EV Battery for Under $15,000
SAIC, a Chinese automaker, has launched its first electric SUV powered by a semi-solid-state EV battery in China. The SUV, which starts at under $15,000, is a significant development in the EV market, as it offers a affordable and efficient option for consumers. The semi-solid-state EV battery used in the SUV provides improved safety, energy density, and charging speed compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. This launch is seen as a major step forward for SAIC, as it looks to expand its presence in the EV market. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/27/15k-electric-suv-semi-solid-state-ev-battery/)

## 03 BYD’s New Yuan Plus EV Adds More Range and Fast Charging for $18,000
BYD, a leading Chinese automaker, has launched the third-generation Yuan Plus, sold overseas as the Atto 3. The new EV features BYD's new Flash Charging technology, which allows for nearly 400 miles of driving range. The Yuan Plus is priced at under $18,000, making it an attractive option for consumers looking for an affordable and efficient EV. The new Flash Charging technology used in the Yuan Plus provides faster charging speeds and improved energy density, making it a significant development in the EV market.
