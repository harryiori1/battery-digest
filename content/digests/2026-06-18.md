## 01 GM Partners with Redwood for Full Battery Lifecycle Management
General Motors has expanded its partnership with Redwood Materials to cover every stage of the battery lifecycle, including manufacturing scrap recovery, end-of-life recycling, and second-life energy storage. This partnership marks a significant step in GM's efforts to reduce waste and increase the sustainability of its battery supply chain. The latest piece of the partnership is a 1.5 MW / 7.2 MWh energy storage system built from roughly 100 repurposed GM battery packs. This system will be deployed at a GM factory, demonstrating the potential for second-life energy storage applications. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/06/09/gm-first-automaker-redwood-materials-full-battery-lifecycle/)

## 02 China's Sodium-Ion Push Reaches Heavy Trucks with FAW Jiefang Trial
FAW Jiefang, a leading Chinese heavy truck manufacturer, has begun trials of sodium-ion batteries in its vehicles. This marks a significant step in China's push for sodium-ion technology, which is seen as a potential alternative to traditional lithium-ion batteries. Sodium-ion batteries have the potential to be more cost-effective and sustainable than lithium-ion batteries, making them an attractive option for heavy-duty applications. The trial is a key part of China's efforts to develop a domestic sodium-ion battery industry, with several companies already investing in research and development. [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/06/18/china-sodium-ion-heavy-trucks-faw-jiefang-trial/)

## 03 Deutsche Bank to Raise Funds for Lithium-Electric New Materials Integration Project
Deutsche Bank plans to raise up to 2.9 billion yuan for a lithium-electric new materials integration project, including a 200,000-ton phosphoric acid new materials project. The project aims to increase the production of new-generation high-pressure phosphoric acid new materials, which are used in the production of lithium-ion batteries. The investment is seen as a key part of China's efforts to develop a domestic lithium-ion battery industry, with several companies already investing in research and development.
