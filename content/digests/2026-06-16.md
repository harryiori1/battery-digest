## 01 Huawei HIMA Brings in Battery Suppliers Beyond CATL
Huawei's electric vehicle arm, HIMA, is expanding its battery supply chain to reduce costs. According to a report by CnEVPost, HIMA is bringing in battery suppliers beyond CATL, which has been its primary battery supplier. This move is seen as a cost-cutting push, as HIMA aims to reduce its reliance on a single supplier and negotiate better prices with multiple suppliers. The report does not specify which new suppliers HIMA is working with, but it is expected that the company will announce more details in the coming months. This development is significant for the battery industry, as it highlights the growing importance of diversifying supply chains and reducing costs in the electric vehicle market. ## 02 Tesla Cybercab EPA Specs Revealed: 48 kWh Battery Pack
The Tesla Cybercab's technical specifications have been revealed, including its battery pack and motor power. According to a report by Electrek, the Cybercab has a 48 kWh battery pack and a 219 HP motor. The vehicle's curb weight is 3,113 lbs, and it has a preliminary range of over 200 miles. The report is based on EPA certification documents, which provide detailed information about the vehicle's technical specifications. The Cybercab is Tesla's first front-wheel-drive vehicle, and its specs are expected to be competitive with other electric vehicles in its class. The revelation of the Cybercab's specs is significant for the battery industry, as it highlights the ongoing development of electric vehicles with increasingly efficient battery packs.
