## 01 Svolt to Mass-Produce Hybrid Solid-Liquid Batteries by September
Svolt, a Chinese battery manufacturer, has announced plans to mass-produce hybrid solid-liquid batteries by September. The company aims to produce these batteries at the same cost as liquid batteries, which could significantly reduce the cost of energy storage. Hybrid solid-liquid batteries combine the benefits of solid-state batteries, such as improved safety and energy density, with the cost-effectiveness of liquid batteries. Svolt's production plans are set to begin in September, with the company targeting a wide range of applications, including electric vehicles and renewable energy systems. This development could have a significant impact on the energy storage industry, enabling the widespread adoption of hybrid solid-liquid batteries. ## 02 BYD's New Blade Battery Put to the Test with 2,700-Mile Drive
BYD, a Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer, is putting its new Blade battery to the test with a 2,700-mile drive across China's largest expressway. The drive is designed to demonstrate the battery's range and charging capabilities, including its 5-minute Flash Charging technology. The Blade battery is a key component of BYD's electric vehicles, and the company is confident that it can withstand the demands of long-distance driving. The test drive is set to begin soon, with the company expecting to complete it in a short period. This development could have a significant impact on the electric vehicle industry, enabling the widespread adoption of BYD's Blade battery technology. ## 03 NLR Battery Innovation Awarded NASA’s Invention of the Year
NLR Battery Innovation has been awarded NASA's Invention of the Year for its lithium-ion battery technology used in the Artemis II mission. The battery technology was developed in collaboration with NASA researchers and has been recognized for its innovative design and performance. The award is a significant recognition of the company's contributions to the field of energy storage and its potential to enable future space missions. The lithium-ion battery technology used in the Artemis II mission is designed to provide high-energy density and long cycle life, making it an ideal choice for space applications. This development could have a significant impact on the space industry, enabling the widespread adoption of lithium-ion battery technology in future missions.
