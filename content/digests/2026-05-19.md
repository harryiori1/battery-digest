## 01 Svolt to Mass-Produce Hybrid Solid-Liquid Batteries by September
Svolt, a Chinese battery manufacturer, plans to mass-produce hybrid solid-liquid batteries by September at liquid battery costs. This breakthrough in battery technology could significantly reduce production costs and increase the efficiency of electric vehicles. The hybrid solid-liquid battery combines the benefits of solid-state batteries, such as improved safety and energy density, with the cost-effectiveness of liquid batteries. Svolt's innovative approach could disrupt the battery industry and accelerate the adoption of electric vehicles. With the ability to produce hybrid solid-liquid batteries at scale, Svolt is poised to become a major player in the global battery market. ## 02 BYD's New Blade Battery Put to the Test with 2,700-Mile Drive
BYD is putting its new Blade Battery to the test with a 2,700-mile drive across China's largest expressway. The new Song Ultra EV, equipped with the Blade Battery and 5-minute Flash Charging tech, will become the first electric vehicle to complete the full trip. This ambitious undertaking demonstrates BYD's confidence in its new battery technology and its commitment to pushing the boundaries of electric vehicle performance. The Blade Battery is designed to provide improved safety, energy density, and charging speed, making it an attractive option for electric vehicle manufacturers. With the success of this test drive, BYD is likely to gain a competitive edge in the global electric vehicle market. [Source: Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/18/byd-drives-ev-2700-miles-to-prove-new-battery-5-min-charging/)

## 03 Honda and LG Partner on Battery-Swapping Electric Motorcycles
Honda and LG Energy Solution are partnering to expand battery-swapping electric motorcycles in Vietnam. The new pilot project, launching in Hanoi later this year, aims to provide a convenient and efficient way for motorcycle owners to swap batteries, reducing charging time and increasing range. This innovative approach could revolutionize the motorcycle industry and provide a sustainable alternative to traditional gasoline-powered vehicles. With the growing demand for electric vehicles, Honda and LG's partnership is a significant step towards reducing carbon emissions and promoting eco-friendly transportation. The success of this project could pave the way for similar initiatives in other countries and markets. [Source: Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/18/honda-and-lg-team-up-to-accelerate-battery-swapping-electric-motorcycles/)
