## 01 Nissan’s First EV Powered by Solid-State Batteries On Track for 2028
Nissan has announced that it is on track to launch its first electric vehicles powered by solid-state batteries by 2028. This milestone marks a significant step forward in the company's electrification efforts, as solid-state batteries offer improved safety, range, and charging speeds compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. Nissan's solid-state battery technology is expected to provide a range of over 500 miles on a single charge, with charging times reduced to just 10 minutes. The company's commitment to solid-state batteries demonstrates its focus on innovation and customer satisfaction, as it aims to become a leader in the electric vehicle market. ## 02 CATL to Unveil Sodium Battery and Fast-Charging Tech at Apr 21 Tech Day
CATL, a leading battery manufacturer, will unveil its sodium battery and fast-charging technology at its upcoming tech day on April 21. The company's sodium battery technology is expected to offer improved performance, safety, and sustainability compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. CATL's fast-charging technology will also be showcased, allowing for charging speeds of up to 500 kW. The company's innovations in battery technology demonstrate its commitment to driving the adoption of electric vehicles and renewable energy. ## 03 Samsung SDI Receives First EV Battery Order from Mercedes-Benz, Signs Multi-Year Contract
Samsung SDI has received its first electric vehicle battery order from Mercedes-Benz, signing a multi-year contract to supply the German automaker with electric vehicle batteries. This deal marks a significant milestone for Samsung SDI, as it expands its customer base in the electric vehicle market. The company's high-performance batteries will be used in Mercedes-Benz's upcoming electric vehicles, providing improved range, safety, and charging speeds.
