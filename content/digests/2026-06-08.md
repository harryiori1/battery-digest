## 01 Nissan Joins Solid-State EV Battery Project
Nissan has joined a new solid-state EV battery project, aiming to develop cheaper than China solid-state EV batteries. The project is a collaboration with Gelion, a company that has developed a low-cost, solid-state EV battery technology. The goal of the project is to level the playing field with China, which has been dominating the EV battery market. The new battery technology has the potential to significantly reduce the cost of EV batteries, making them more competitive with traditional gasoline-powered vehicles. With a three-year collaboration, Nissan and Gelion plan to advance the development of this technology and bring it to market. ## 02 Ganfeng Lithium's Energy Storage Cells Fully Produced and Sold
Ganfeng Lithium, a leading lithium producer, has announced that its energy storage cells are fully produced and sold. The company's 588Ah production line will start climbing production in the third quarter, with plans to gradually increase production by the end of the year. Ganfeng Lithium's energy storage cells have been in high demand, with the company's production capacity utilization rate maintaining a high level. The company's self-developed 588Ah and 648Ah large cells are considered industry benchmark products, with higher energy density compared to traditional products. ## 03 Solid State Battery with 3500Wh/kg Energy Density
A new breakthrough in solid-state battery technology has been reported, with a solid-state battery achieving an energy density of 3500Wh/kg. This is a significant improvement over current lithium-ion batteries, which have an average energy density of around 250Wh/kg. The new battery technology has the potential to significantly increase the range and efficiency of electric vehicles, making them more competitive with traditional gasoline-powered vehicles. While the details of the new battery technology are not yet available, the achievement of such a high energy density is a significant milestone in the development of solid-state batteries.
