## 01 CATL Unveils Fully Redundant Chassis for Autonomous Vehicles
CATL, a leading battery manufacturer, has unveiled its fully redundant chassis, dubbed the "Rock-Solid L4," at the Beijing Auto Show. This innovation is a significant step forward in the company's efforts to develop advanced battery and automotive technologies for autonomous vehicles. The Rock-Solid L4 chassis is designed to provide a high level of safety and reliability, with redundant systems ensuring that the vehicle can continue to operate even in the event of a component failure. This development demonstrates CATL's commitment to pushing the boundaries of battery technology and its potential applications in the automotive industry. ## 02 California Senate Bill Could Turn Home Batteries into Moneymakers
A new bill proposed in the California senate, SB 913, could enable homeowners to turn their home backup batteries into revenue-generating assets. The bill would allow utilities and grid operators to treat home batteries as virtual power plants, enabling them to sell excess energy back to the grid during peak demand periods. This could create new opportunities for homeowners to monetize their energy storage systems and contribute to a more resilient and decentralized energy grid. The proposal has the potential to accelerate the adoption of home battery systems and promote a more efficient use of energy resources.
