## 01 CATL Signs 60 GWh Sodium-Ion Battery Deal with HyperStrong
CATL, the world's largest battery maker, has signed a massive 60 GWh sodium-ion battery deal with energy storage integrator HyperStrong. This deal marks a significant milestone in the commercialization of sodium-ion batteries, which are seen as a potential alternative to lithium-ion batteries. The three-year agreement is equivalent to half of all energy storage batteries CATL delivered in 2025. CATL claims that this deal proves that it has "overcome the challenges of the entire sodium-ion battery mass production process". This development is significant for the battery industry, as it indicates that sodium-ion batteries are becoming increasingly mainstream. ## 02 BYD Sells Most Expensive EV Ever for Nearly $3 Million
BYD has sold its most expensive EV ever, the Yangwang U9 Xtreme, for nearly $3 million at the Beijing Auto Show. The Yangwang U9 Xtreme is a 3,000 hp electric supercar that boasts impressive performance capabilities. This sale marks a significant milestone for BYD, as it demonstrates the company's ability to compete in the high-end EV market. The sale of this vehicle also highlights the growing demand for luxury EVs, which is driving innovation and investment in the industry. ## 03 Accelergen’s 300 MW East Side Energy Storage Project Approved
The Unified Government of Wyandotte County has approved Accelergen’s proposed 300 MW East Side Energy Storage project, which will be one of the largest utility-scale energy storage systems in Kansas. This project is significant for the energy storage industry, as it demonstrates the growing demand for energy storage solutions. The project will provide a range of benefits, including improved grid stability and increased renewable energy penetration. The approval of this project also highlights the importance of energy storage in enabling the transition to a low-carbon economy.
