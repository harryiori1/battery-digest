## 01 Ride1Up Launches World's First E-Bike with Semi-Solid State Battery
Ride1Up, a San Diego-based electric bicycle maker, has launched the world's first e-bike with a semi-solid state battery, the Ride1Up Revv1 EVO. This technological shift is expected to bring significant advantages to e-bikes, including improved safety and longevity. The semi-solid state battery is a major innovation in the e-bike industry, and Ride1Up's move is seen as a significant milestone. The company's decision to adopt semi-solid state battery technology is likely to influence the direction of the e-bike market, with other manufacturers potentially following suit. As the e-bike market continues to grow, the adoption of semi-solid state batteries could become a key differentiator for manufacturers. ## 02 BYD April New Energy Vehicle Production Reaches 322,300 Units
BYD, a leading Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer, has announced its April production and sales figures for new energy vehicles. The company produced 322,300 units and sold 321,100 units in April, with a cumulative production of 1,030,300 units and sales of 1,021,600 units for the year. However, BYD's sales and production declined by 26.02% and 28.56% year-over-year, respectively. The company also reported that its new energy vehicle battery installation reached 20.98 GWh in April, with a cumulative installation of 81.19 GWh for the year. ## 03 BYD's New Energy Vehicle Battery Installation Reaches 20.98 GWh in April
BYD's new energy vehicle battery installation has reached 20.98 GWh in April, with a cumulative installation of 81.19 GWh for the year.
