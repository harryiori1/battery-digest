## 01 GM Partners with Redwood Materials Across Full Battery Lifecycle
General Motors has expanded its partnership with Redwood Materials to cover every stage of the battery lifecycle — manufacturing scrap recovery, end-of-life recycling, and now second-life energy storage deployed at a GM factory. This partnership makes GM the first automaker to hit all three with JB Straubel’s company. The latest piece is a 1.5 MW / 7.2 MWh energy storage system built from roughly 100 repurposed GM batteries. This collaboration aims to reduce waste, lower costs, and increase the sustainability of battery production. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/06/09/gm-first-automaker-redwood-materials-full-battery-lifecycle/)

## 02 General Motors to Develop Sodium-Ion Battery Cells in the US
General Motors is betting on sodium-ion technology for grid-scale battery storage, with plans to develop and manufacture cells in the US. The company has partnered with Peak Energy to develop and deploy next-generation sodium-ion battery cells for grid-scale energy storage. GM will be responsible for the research and development of the sodium-ion cells at its battery lab in Michigan, while Peak Energy will integrate the cells into its proprietary energy storage systems. This move marks a significant step towards the commercialization of sodium-ion batteries. [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/news/798154/general-motors-sodium-ion-battery-production/)

## 03 Longpan Technology to Invest 10.88 Billion Yuan in Indonesian Lithium Project
Longpan Technology plans to construct a lithium project in Indonesia, with a total investment of approximately 1.6 billion USD. The project aims to produce 120,000 tons of lithium iron phosphate cathode materials per year. This investment is expected to increase the company's production capacity and meet the growing demand for lithium-ion batteries.
