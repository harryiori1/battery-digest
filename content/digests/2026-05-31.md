## 01 ProLogium to Go Public Through Merger to Fund Solid-State Battery Growth
ProLogium, a leading solid-state EV battery maker, has announced that it will become a publicly listed company through a business merger with a special purpose acquisition company (SPAC). This move is expected to provide the necessary funding for ProLogium's next growth stage in solid-state EV battery production. As the first company to commercialize solid-state batteries, ProLogium's decision to go public is a significant development in the EV battery industry. With the demand for solid-state batteries on the rise, ProLogium's expansion plans are likely to have a major impact on the market. ## 02 CATL's Sodium-Ion Battery to be Mass-Produced on a Large Scale This Year
CATL's sodium-ion battery is set to be mass-produced this year, offering non-flammable, non-explosive, and cold-resistant properties. This marks a significant advancement in EV battery technology, as sodium-ion batteries have the potential to be more cost-effective and environmentally friendly than traditional lithium-ion batteries. ## 03 Ganfeng Lithium Achieves Milestone with 10 Ah Solid-State Batteries and 500 Wh/kg Energy Density
Ganfeng Lithium has begun producing the world's first 10 Ah solid-state batteries with an energy density of 500 Wh/kg. This major milestone in the development of solid-state EV batteries is expected to have a significant impact on the industry. With supply deals with major OEMs such as Tesla, Volkswagen, and Hyundai, Ganfeng Lithium is well-positioned to play a leading role in the solid-state battery market.
