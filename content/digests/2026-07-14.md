## 01 BYD Overhauls Overseas Brand Structure, Bets on Channel Integration and Flash Charging Network
BYD is restructuring its overseas brand, focusing on integrated channels and flash charging networks to enhance its global presence. This move is expected to improve the company's competitiveness in the international market. According to [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/07/15/byd-overhauls-overseas-brand-structure/), BYD aims to create a more streamlined and efficient brand structure, which will enable the company to better respond to changing market demands. The integration of channels and flash charging networks will also facilitate the adoption of BYD's electric vehicles, making them more appealing to a wider range of customers.

## 02 Malaysia to Start Producing Locally Developed Electric Vehicle Batteries in July
Malaysia is set to begin local production of electric vehicle batteries, marking a significant step in its EV industry development. As reported by [第一电动](https://www.d1ev.com/news/shichang/306935), the country will start producing locally developed electric vehicle batteries in July, which will help reduce reliance on imported batteries and enhance the competitiveness of Malaysian-made electric vehicles. This development is expected to have a positive impact on the local economy and contribute to the growth of the country's EV sector.

## 03 The 10 Top Battery Integrators in the World Revealed
A new report by Wood Mackenzie identifies the top 10 battery integrators globally, although specific details and rankings are not disclosed. According to [CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/07/14/the-10-top-battery-integrators-in-the-world/), the report provides insight into the companies that are leading the way in battery integration, which is a critical aspect of the energy storage industry. The report's findings are expected to be of interest to industry stakeholders, as they will provide a better understanding of the competitive landscape and the key players in the market.
