## 01 CATL Developing 12,000 Wh Per Kg Lithium-Air Battery
CATL, a leading battery manufacturer, has announced the development of lithium-air batteries with an energy density of up to 12,000 Wh/kg. This breakthrough in battery technology has the potential to significantly increase the range and efficiency of electric vehicles. Lithium-air batteries, also known as lithium-oxygen batteries, have been in development for several years, but CATL's achievement is a major milestone in the field. The company's lithium-air batteries could potentially be used in a wide range of applications, from electric vehicles to renewable energy systems. ## 02 Honeycomb Energy's Power Battery Loading Volume Grows 37.2% YoY
Honeycomb Energy, a Chinese battery manufacturer, has reported a significant increase in its power battery loading volume, with a year-over-year growth of 37.2% in January-April. The company's total loading volume reached 9.3GWh, ranking 9th globally. Honeycomb Energy's growth is attributed to its increasing presence in the global market, with its products being used in a wide range of applications, from electric vehicles to energy storage systems. ## 03 GM Pitches New LMR EV Battery On Earth, New EV On The Moon
GM, a leading automaker, has announced the development of a new LMR EV battery, which will be used in its upcoming electric vehicles. The company has also announced a collaboration with NASA on a lunar rover project, which will utilize a new EV battery technology. The LMR EV battery is designed to be more efficient and have a longer range than current EV batteries. The lunar rover project is part of NASA's plans to return humans to the moon by 2025, and GM's EV battery technology will play a critical role in the mission.
