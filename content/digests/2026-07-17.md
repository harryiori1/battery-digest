## 01 Hyundai Opens $5 Billion Battery Plant in Joint Facility with SK On
Hyundai has opened a new $5 billion battery plant in a joint facility with SK On, marking a significant step towards becoming America's #2 EV brand. The plant is expected to produce batteries for Hyundai's electric vehicles, including the Ioniq 5 and the Genesis GV60. With this new plant, Hyundai aims to increase its EV production capacity and reduce its reliance on external battery suppliers. The joint facility with SK On will also enable Hyundai to develop more advanced battery technologies and improve its competitiveness in the EV market. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/07/15/hyundai-opens-5b-battery-plant-push-for-americas-2-ev-brand/)

## 02 Tesla’s LFP Battery Holds Charge Better, Study Finds
A new analysis of nearly 10,000 real-world EV battery tests has found that Tesla's lithium iron phosphate (LFP) battery holds its charge better than nickel-based versions. The study found that the Tesla Model 3 with an LFP battery pack averaged 93.3% battery health among cars that had driven more than 62,000 miles, outperforming all nickel-based versions of the car. This suggests that LFP batteries may be a more durable and long-lasting option for EVs. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/07/15/tesla-lfp-battery-outlasts-nickel-model-3/)

## 03 Hunan Yueneng to Invest 24 Billion Yuan in Battery Material Recycling Project
Hunan Yueneng plans to invest 24 billion yuan in a battery material recycling project in Guizhou Province. The project aims to construct a mineralized new energy battery material recycling industry project, which will include the production of 800,000 tons of lithium iron phosphate and 1 million tons of phosphoric acid. The project is expected to be completed in 5 years and will help to improve the recycling of battery materials and reduce waste. [36氪](https://36kr.com/newsflashes/3899699021465223?f=rss)
