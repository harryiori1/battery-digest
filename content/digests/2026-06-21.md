## 01 BYD Battery Production Ramps Up In Brazil
BYD is expanding its battery production in Brazil to support the growing demand for electric vehicles (EVs) outside of China. The company's vehicle exports have grown by 65% this year, with an 80% increase in May. This move is part of BYD's strategy to increase its global presence and return to EV sales growth. The Brazilian facility will play a crucial role in supplying batteries to BYD's EV manufacturing plants in the region. As the demand for EVs continues to rise, BYD's investment in battery production will help the company to stay competitive in the market. ## 02 India Needs 10 GWh of Battery Storage to Stop Renewable Energy Curtailment
India requires approximately 10 gigawatt-hours (GWh) of battery storage to prevent renewable energy curtailment caused by coal-based power plants' inability to ramp down during peak solar hours. The country's midday solar surge often leads to renewable energy waste, as coal-based power plants cannot adjust their output quickly enough. Battery storage can help mitigate this issue by storing excess energy generated during peak solar hours and releasing it when needed. This would not only reduce renewable energy waste but also promote a more efficient and sustainable energy mix. [CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/2026/06/20/india-needs-10-gwh-of-battery-storage-now-to-stop-coals-inflexibility-wasting-clean-power/)

## 03 Tesla Model Y Battery Retains Only 88% Capacity After 20,000km
A real-world test of a Tesla Model Y has revealed significant battery degradation after just 20,000 kilometers. The vehicle's battery retained only 88% of its original capacity, raising concerns about the long-term durability of Tesla's batteries. This finding is particularly noteworthy, as it suggests that Tesla's batteries may not be as resilient as expected, potentially impacting the overall performance and range of the vehicle.
