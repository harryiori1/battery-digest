## 01 GM Partners with Peak Energy to Develop Sodium-Ion Battery Cells
General Motors (GM) has partnered with Peak Energy to develop sodium-ion battery cells for grid-scale energy storage projects. This move marks a significant investment in alternative battery chemistries, as GM aims to reduce its reliance on lithium-ion batteries. The partnership will enable GM to develop more sustainable and cost-effective energy storage solutions. According to the announcement, GM Ventures is making a strategic investment in Peak Energy to support the development of sodium-ion battery cells. This technology has the potential to offer improved performance, safety, and affordability compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. The collaboration between GM and Peak Energy is expected to accelerate the adoption of sodium-ion batteries in the energy storage market. ## 02 BYD to Bring 5-Minute Flash Charging to Canada
BYD is planning to bring its 5-minute Flash Charging network to Canada, enabling electric vehicle (EV) owners to charge their cars quickly and efficiently. The Flash Charging network, which can add about 250 miles (400 km) of range in 5 minutes, is expected to be deployed in Canada, marking the first confirmed deployment in North America. BYD has posted a job listing for a manager to oversee the expansion of its Flash Charging network in Canada, indicating the company's commitment to investing in the region's EV infrastructure. The introduction of Flash Charging in Canada is likely to boost the adoption of EVs, as it addresses one of the primary concerns of potential buyers: charging time.
