## 01 GM Partners with Peak Energy to Develop Sodium-Ion Battery Cells
General Motors (GM) has partnered with Peak Energy to develop sodium-ion battery cells for grid-scale energy storage projects. This move marks a significant investment in alternative battery technologies, as GM aims to reduce its reliance on lithium-ion batteries. The partnership will focus on producing sodium-ion battery cells that can be used in large-scale energy storage systems, providing a more sustainable and cost-effective solution for the grid. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/06/10/gm-sodium-ion-battery-peak-energy/), GM Ventures is making a strategic investment in Peak Energy to support the development of this technology.

## 02 China's Power and Other Battery Production Totaled 192GWh in May
China's power and other battery production reached a total of 192GWh in May, representing a 38% increase year-on-year. This growth is attributed to the rising demand for electric vehicles and energy storage systems. As reported by [36氪](https://36kr.com/newsflashes/3852723150525698?f=rss), the country's lithium-ion battery installations reached 72GWh in May, with a total of 259GWh installed from January to May.
