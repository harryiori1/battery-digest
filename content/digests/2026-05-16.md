## 01 Tesla Semi Battery Sizes Confirmed: 822 kWh and 548 kWh
The California Air Resources Board has officially confirmed the Tesla Semi’s battery capacities through a regulatory filing. The Long Range Semi carries an 822 kWh usable battery pack, while the Standard Range comes in at 548 kWh, both using NCMA lithium-ion cells. This confirmation provides clarity on the Tesla Semi's battery capabilities, which are smaller than what Elon Musk originally suggested back in 2022. The 822 kWh and 548 kWh battery packs will power the Tesla Semi, which is expected to have a significant impact on the electric vehicle market. ## 02 Semi-Solid State Batteries Enter E-Bike Industry
After years of hype, semi-solid state batteries are finally being introduced to the e-bike market. This technology promises improved performance, range, and safety compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. The e-bike industry is expected to benefit from the increased efficiency and reduced weight of semi-solid state batteries, making electric bikes more appealing to consumers. As the demand for electric vehicles continues to grow, the adoption of semi-solid state batteries in the e-bike industry could pave the way for their use in other electric vehicles. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/08/semi-solid-state-batteries-are-coming-to-the-e-bike-industry-and-for-real-this-time/)

## 03 Ford’s New Battery Container Takes on Tesla Megapack
Ford Energy has introduced a new battery container designed to compete with Tesla's Megapack. The LFP-powered DC block is designed to last 20 years and packs over 5 megawatt-hours of energy. This new battery container is a significant development in the energy storage market, as it provides a viable alternative to Tesla's Megapack. The Ford Energy battery container is expected to be used in a variety of applications, including renewable energy systems and electric vehicle charging infrastructure.
