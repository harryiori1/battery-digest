## 01 Tesla Invests $250M in Giga Berlin Battery Cells
Tesla has announced a significant investment of $250 million in its Giga Berlin factory to boost battery cell production capacity to 18 GWh per year. This move is expected to create over 1,500 battery-related jobs and demonstrates Tesla's commitment to expanding its electric vehicle manufacturing capabilities in Europe. The investment comes after a successful works council election, which saw Tesla workers opt out of joining the IG Metall union. This development is crucial for the EV industry, as it highlights the growing demand for electric vehicles and the need for increased battery production to meet this demand. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/12/tesla-giga-berlin-250-million-battery-cell-investment-18-gwh/)

## 02 CATL and Yutong Deepen Ties for Global Commercial EV Expansion
CATL, a leading battery manufacturer, has deepened its ties with Yutong, a commercial electric vehicle manufacturer, to accelerate the adoption of electric vehicles in the commercial sector. This partnership aims to promote the development of global commercial electric vehicles and further expand CATL's presence in the market. The collaboration will focus on developing advanced battery technologies and integrating them into Yutong's electric vehicles. This development is significant, as it highlights the growing importance of partnerships in the EV industry and the need for collaboration to drive innovation and adoption. [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/13/catl-yutong-deepen-ties-global-commercial-ev-expansion/)

## 03 BYD in Talks to Take Over Underutilized European Factories
BYD, a leading electric vehicle manufacturer, is in talks with Stellantis and other carmakers to take over underutilized European factories. This potential expansion of BYD's manufacturing capabilities in Europe could significantly increase the company's production capacity and help meet the growing demand for electric vehicles in the region. The talks are still in the early stages, but if successful, they could mark a significant milestone in BYD's global expansion plans. This development is crucial, as it highlights the need for EV manufacturers to expand their production capabilities to meet growing demand and stay competitive in the market.
