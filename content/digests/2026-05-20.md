## 01 BYD Struggles to Meet Demand for New Fast-Charging EVs
BYD's new flagship electric SUV, equipped with the company's new Blade Battery 2.0 and Flash Charging technology, has received over 100,000 orders. Additionally, the new Song Ultra EV has secured over 61,000 orders. However, BYD has stated that it is struggling to meet the high demand for these new models. This surge in demand is a testament to the growing popularity of electric vehicles, particularly those with advanced fast-charging capabilities. The Blade Battery 2.0 is a significant improvement over its predecessor, offering enhanced safety, range, and charging speeds. As the demand for electric vehicles continues to rise, manufacturers like BYD will need to ramp up production to meet the growing needs of the market. ## 02 Ganfeng Lithium Starts Small-Scale Production of 500 Wh/kg Solid-State Batteries
Ganfeng Lithium has begun small-scale production of solid-state batteries with an energy density of 500 Wh/kg. This milestone marks a significant breakthrough in battery technology, as solid-state batteries have the potential to offer improved safety, range, and charging speeds compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. The production of these high-energy density batteries is a crucial step towards the widespread adoption of electric vehicles and other applications that require advanced battery technology. As the industry continues to evolve, companies like Ganfeng Lithium are playing a vital role in driving innovation and pushing the boundaries of what is possible with battery technology. [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/21/ganfeng-starts-small-scale-production-500-wh-kg-solid-state-batteries/)

## 03 BYD Launches its First Plug-in Hybrid MPV M6 DM in Indonesia
BYD has launched its first plug-in hybrid MPV, the M6 DM, in the Indonesian market. This new model expands BYD's product lineup and offers customers a unique combination of electric and gasoline power. The M6 DM is equipped with BYD's advanced battery technology, providing a range of up to 80 km on electric power alone. This launch marks a significant milestone for BYD in the Indonesian market, as the company continues to expand its presence and offer a wider range of electric and hybrid vehicles to customers.
