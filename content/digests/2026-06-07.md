## 01 Tesla Model Y Battery Remains Healthy After 16,000 Miles Of Mostly Fast Charging
A recent test of a Tesla Model Y's battery after 16,000 miles of mostly fast charging showed that the battery remains very healthy, with minimal degradation. This is a significant finding, as it suggests that Tesla's batteries can withstand the rigors of frequent fast charging without suffering significant losses in capacity or performance. The test, which was conducted over a period of six months, found that the battery's capacity had decreased by only a small amount, despite being fast-charged for the majority of its mileage. This is a testament to the quality and durability of Tesla's battery technology, and should provide reassurance to owners of Tesla vehicles who frequently use fast charging. According to [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/news/798001/tesla-model-y-battery-degradation/), the test results demonstrate the reliability and longevity of Tesla's batteries.

## 02 BYD Exports Rose 80% Year Over Year In May, Driven By Growing Global Demand

This increase in exports is also a significant boost to BYD's bottom line, and demonstrates the company's commitment to expanding its global presence.

## 03 Waymo Partners With B2U To Repurpose Retired Self-Driving Taxi Batteries For Grid Energy Storage
Waymo has partnered with B2U to repurpose retired self-driving taxi batteries for grid energy storage, reducing waste and increasing sustainability. This innovative partnership is a significant step forward in the development of sustainable energy storage solutions, and demonstrates the potential for retired EV batteries to be repurposed for other uses. According to [第一电动](https://www.d1ev.com/news/shichang/302201), the partnership will see Waymo's retired self-driving taxi batteries being repurposed for use in grid energy storage applications, reducing the need for new battery production and minimizing waste. This is a significant development in the field of sustainable energy storage, and demonstrates the potential for creative partnerships to drive innovation and reduce environmental impact.
