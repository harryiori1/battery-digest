## 01 Tesla Model Y Battery Tested After 16,000 Miles Of Mostly Fast Charging
A recent test on a Tesla Model Y's battery has shown that it remains healthy after 16,000 miles of mostly fast charging. The test, conducted over six months, found that the battery had minimal degradation, with its capacity and performance remaining relatively consistent. This is a significant finding, as it suggests that Tesla's battery technology can withstand the rigors of frequent fast charging. The test results will be welcome news for Tesla owners and potential buyers, who may have concerns about the impact of fast charging on battery longevity. According to the test, the Model Y's battery retained around 95% of its original capacity after 16,000 miles, with the vehicle still able to achieve its advertised range. ## 02 BYD's Cast Aluminum Frame Is Lighter, Tougher, and Safer Than Steel
BYD has announced that its cast aluminum frame is lighter, tougher, and safer than traditional steel frames. The company claims that its aluminum frame, which underpins its new Yangwang U8L SUV, can withstand a 12-ton lift test while weighing over 100 pounds less than a comparable steel frame. This is a significant achievement, as it suggests that BYD's frame is not only stronger but also more efficient. The use of cast aluminum frames could potentially become a new standard in the automotive industry, as manufacturers seek to reduce weight and improve safety. BYD's frame is also designed to be more resistant to corrosion, which could help to extend the lifespan of its vehicles. ## 03 Liquid Cooling Key to Withstanding Continuous High Power in Super-Charging Stations
Traditional super-charging stations are struggling to withstand continuous high power, with many experiencing overheating and reduced performance. However, a new solution is emerging in the form of liquid cooling. By using liquid cooling systems, super-charging stations can potentially increase their power output and reduce the risk of overheating. This is a significant development, as it could help to improve the efficiency and reliability of super-charging infrastructure. Liquid cooling systems work by circulating a liquid coolant through the charging equipment, which helps to absorb and dissipate heat.
