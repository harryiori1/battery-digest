## 01 Tesla Model Y Battery Tested After 16,000 Miles Of Mostly Fast Charging
A recent test on a Tesla Model Y's battery after 16,000 miles of mostly fast charging has shown that the battery remains very healthy, with minimal degradation. This is a significant finding, as it suggests that Tesla's batteries can withstand the rigors of frequent fast charging without suffering significant losses in capacity or overall health. The test, which was conducted over a period of six months, found that the battery's state of charge and overall health were still well within acceptable limits. This is good news for Tesla owners who rely on fast charging to keep their vehicles on the road. According to [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/news/798001/tesla-model-y-battery-degradation/), the test results demonstrate the robustness of Tesla's battery technology.

## 02 BYD Says Its Cast Aluminum Frame Is Lighter, Tougher, and Safer Than Steel
BYD has announced that its cast aluminum frame is a better option than traditional steel frames, weighing over 100 lbs less while still passing a brutal 12-ton lift test. This is a significant development, as it could lead to the widespread adoption of aluminum frames in the automotive industry. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/06/02/byd-says-its-cast-aluminum-frame-is-lighter-tougher-and-safer-than-steel/), BYD's cast aluminum frame is not only lighter but also tougher and safer than traditional steel frames.

## 03 Why Traditional Super-Charging Stations Can't Withstand Continuous High Power
A recent article has highlighted the limitations of traditional super-charging stations, which are unable to withstand continuous high power due to the lack of liquid cooling. Liquid cooling is a critical component in enabling super-charging stations to operate at high power levels for extended periods. According to [第一电动](https://www.d1ev.com/news/shichang/301910), the answer to this problem lies in the adoption of liquid cooling technology, which can help to mitigate the heat generated by high-power charging.
