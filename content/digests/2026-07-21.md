## 01 CATL Signs 2GWh Sodium Battery Storage Deal with Europe's Solarpro
CATL has signed a significant deal with Solarpro to provide 2GWh of sodium battery storage, marking a major milestone in the adoption of sodium-ion technology. This deal highlights the growing interest in sodium-ion batteries as a potential alternative to traditional lithium-ion batteries. Sodium-ion batteries offer several advantages, including lower costs, improved safety, and reduced dependence on critical minerals like lithium, cobalt, and nickel. The deal is expected to play a key role in the development of Europe's energy storage market, with Solarpro planning to use the sodium battery storage systems to provide backup power for its solar farms. ## 02 Janus Electric to Convert 67 Diesel Trucks to Battery-Swap EVs
Janus Electric has received an order to convert 67 heavy-duty diesel trucks to battery-swap electric vehicles, highlighting the growing demand for sustainable transportation solutions. The conversion process will involve replacing the diesel engines with advanced electric powertrains and installing battery-swap systems, allowing the trucks to operate for extended periods without the need for recharging. This deal marks a significant milestone in the adoption of electric vehicles in the heavy-duty trucking industry, with Janus Electric poised to play a key role in the transition to sustainable transportation. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/07/20/order-up-janus-electric-set-to-convert-67-diesel-trucks-to-battery-swap-evs/)

## 03 Honeycomb Energy's Hubei Daye Factory Produces its First PACK Battery Pack
Honeycomb Energy has announced that its Hubei Daye factory has produced its first PACK battery pack, marking a significant milestone in the company's production capabilities. The PACK battery pack is a critical component in electric vehicles, providing the energy storage needed to power the vehicle. The production of the first PACK battery pack at the Hubei Daye factory highlights Honeycomb Energy's commitment to developing its manufacturing capabilities and meeting the growing demand for electric vehicle batteries.
