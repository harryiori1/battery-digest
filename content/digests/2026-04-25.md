## 01 Nissan Completes Performance Testing of Solid-State Battery Pack Prototype
Nissan has completed performance testing of its solid-state battery pack prototype, a significant milestone in the development of its electric vehicle technology. This achievement brings the company closer to its goal of launching solid-state battery-powered electric vehicles by 2028. The testing process involved evaluating the battery pack's performance, safety, and durability. With the successful completion of this testing phase, Nissan is now one step closer to mass-producing solid-state batteries, which are expected to offer improved range, charging speed, and overall efficiency. ## 03 Tesla Lithium Refinery Discharge Contains Toxic Metals, Faces Cease-and-Desist Order
Tesla's lithium refinery in Robstown, Texas, has been found to discharge wastewater containing toxic metals, including hexavalent chromium, arsenic, and elevated levels of lithium. The Nueces County Drainage District No. 2, which manages the ditch receiving Tesla's 231,000-gallon daily discharge, has issued a cease-and-desist letter demanding the company to halt its operations. The discovery of toxic metals in the refinery's discharge has raised concerns about the potential environmental and health impacts. Tesla must now take immediate action to address these issues and ensure that its operations comply with environmental regulations.
