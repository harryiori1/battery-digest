## 01 ProLogium to Go Public Through Merger to Fund Solid-State Battery Growth
ProLogium, a leading solid-state battery manufacturer, has announced plans to become a publicly listed company through a merger with a special purpose acquisition company (SPAC). This move is expected to provide the necessary funding for ProLogium to accelerate its growth stage and further develop its solid-state battery technology. As the first company to commercialize solid-state batteries, ProLogium has established itself as a pioneer in the industry. The merger is seen as a significant step forward for the company, enabling it to expand its production capacity and meet the growing demand for solid-state batteries in the electric vehicle (EV) market. With this development, ProLogium is poised to play a major role in shaping the future of EV battery technology. ## 02 SAIC Launches Electric SUV with Semi-Solid-State EV Battery for Under $15,000
SAIC has introduced a new electric SUV in China, featuring a semi-solid-state EV battery, priced at less than $15,000. This affordable electric vehicle is expected to appeal to a wide range of consumers, making it an attractive option for those looking to transition to electric mobility. The semi-solid-state battery technology used in this SUV offers improved safety, energy density, and charging speeds compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries. With a starting price of under $15,000, SAIC's new electric SUV is set to disrupt the Chinese EV market, which is already experiencing rapid growth. As the demand for affordable and efficient EVs continues to rise, SAIC's latest offering is well-positioned to capitalize on this trend. ## 03 Ganfeng Lithium Achieves Milestone with 10 Ah Solid-State Batteries and 500 Wh/kg Energy Density
Ganfeng Lithium, a leading Chinese lithium metal giant, has begun producing the world's first 10 Ah solid-state batteries with an energy density of 500 Wh/kg. This significant milestone marks a major breakthrough in battery technology, offering improved safety, energy density, and charging speeds. Ganfeng Lithium's solid-state batteries have the potential to revolutionize the EV industry, enabling the development of more efficient and longer-range electric vehicles. With supply deals in place with major OEMs such as Tesla, Volkswagen, and Hyundai, Ganfeng Lithium is well-positioned to play a key role in the global EV battery market. As the demand for solid-state batteries continues to grow, Ganfeng Lithium's achievement is expected to have a profound impact on the industry.
