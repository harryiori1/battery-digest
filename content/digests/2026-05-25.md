## 01 CATL Launches China's First Standardized Light Truck Battery Swap Ecosystem
CATL, a leading Chinese battery manufacturer, has launched the country's first standardized light truck battery swap ecosystem. This innovative system is designed to provide a convenient and efficient way for commercial vehicle operators to replace depleted batteries with fully charged ones, reducing downtime and increasing overall productivity. The ecosystem includes a network of battery swap stations, where drivers can quickly exchange their batteries, as well as a range of standardized battery products specifically designed for light trucks. This development is expected to have a significant impact on the adoption of electric commercial vehicles in China, as it addresses one of the major concerns of fleet operators: the need for rapid and convenient battery replacement. According to [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/25/catl-launches-chinas-first-standardized-light-truck-battery-swap-ecosystem/), the launch of this ecosystem marks an important milestone in the development of China's electric vehicle industry.

## 02 Over 90% of Onvo L80 Buyers Opt for BaaS Battery Rental
In a significant trend, over 90% of buyers of the Onvo L80 electric vehicle have chosen to opt for the Battery-as-a-Service (BaaS) option. This means that instead of purchasing the battery outright, these customers are renting it on a monthly basis. The BaaS model is gaining popularity in the EV industry, as it provides customers with a more affordable upfront cost and the flexibility to upgrade to new battery technologies as they become available. The high uptake of the BaaS option for the Onvo L80 suggests that this model is likely to become increasingly common in the future. As reported by [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/25/over-90-onvo-l80-buyers-opt-for-baas/), the success of the BaaS model for the Onvo L80 could have significant implications for the wider EV industry.

## 03 BYD Expands Hybrid Lineup with New Sealion 06 DM-i and Song Ultra DM-i
BYD, a leading Chinese electric vehicle manufacturer, has expanded its hybrid lineup with the introduction of two new models: the Sealion 06 DM-i and the Song Ultra DM-i. These vehicles are part of BYD's efforts to provide customers with a range of options for reducing their environmental impact, while also meeting their needs for performance and convenience. The Sealion 06 DM-i and Song Ultra DM-i feature advanced hybrid powertrains, which combine the benefits of electric and conventional propulsion. According to [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/05/25/byd-expands-hybrid-lineup-new-sealion-06-song-ultra-dm-i-this-week/), the launch of these new models demonstrates BYD's commitment to innovation and customer choice in the hybrid electric vehicle segment.
