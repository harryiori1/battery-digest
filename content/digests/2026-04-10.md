## 01 BYD Partners with KFC for 9-Minute EV Charging
BYD has partnered with KFC to offer EV drivers a 9-minute one-stop shop and charge service. This partnership aims to provide a convenient and fast charging experience for EV owners, allowing them to charge their vehicles while they eat. The service is expected to be a game-changer for the EV industry, as it addresses the common concern of long charging times. With this partnership, BYD is poised to further expand its presence in the global EV market. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/09/byd-fast-food-giant-offer-9-minute-ev-charging/), the partnership is a strategic move by BYD to increase its market share and provide a unique experience for its customers.

## 02 Tesla Adds Sunwoda to Global Supply Chain
Tesla has reportedly added China's Sunwoda to its global supply chain in an effort to drive down battery costs. This move is expected to help Tesla reduce its reliance on traditional battery suppliers and increase its competitiveness in the EV market. Sunwoda is a leading battery manufacturer in China, and its partnership with Tesla is seen as a significant development in the EV industry. According to [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/04/09/tesla-adds-sunwoda-battery-to-global-supply-chain/), the partnership is a strategic move by Tesla to improve its supply chain efficiency and reduce costs.

## 03 China's Power Battery Installations Remain Flat in March
Despite a 50% surge in production, China's power battery installations remained flat in March. This trend is seen as a surprise, given the growing demand for EVs in China. However, according to [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/2026/04/10/china-mar-2026-power-battery-installations/), the flat installations may be due to a variety of factors, including changes in government policies and market dynamics. The trend is being closely watched by industry analysts, as it may have implications for the future of the EV industry in China.
