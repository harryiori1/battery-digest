## 01 CATL Launches Sodium-Ion Batteries in EVs with 370+ Miles Range
CATL, a leading battery manufacturer, is launching sodium-ion batteries in electric vehicles (EVs) in 2026, aiming for a range of over 370 miles. This new technology offers a more efficient and sustainable solution for EVs, with the potential to reduce production costs and increase energy density. According to [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/04/22/catl-launching-sodium-ion-batteries-evs-2026/), CATL's sodium-ion batteries will begin launching in passenger EVs later this year, marking a significant milestone in the development of more sustainable EV batteries.

## 02 Kodali to Invest 1.2 Billion Yuan in Lithium Battery Precision Structural Components Project
Kodali, a Chinese company, plans to invest 1.2 billion yuan in the fourth phase of its Jiangsu lithium battery precision structural components project. The project aims to improve the company's production capacity and meet customer demands for high-quality lithium battery components. As reported by [36氪](https://36kr.com/newsflashes/3777913282876672?f=rss), the investment will be used to purchase land, construct factory buildings, and acquire production lines, with an expected annual output value of approximately 20 billion yuan.

## 03 CATL CTO Discusses Super-Charging and Battery Damage, Claims Balance Can Be Achieved
CATL's CTO has discussed the impact of super-charging on batteries, claiming that the company can balance the damage with its new technology. According to [36氪Web](https://36kr.com/p/3777869935727872), CATL's CTO stated that super-charging can damage batteries, but the company has developed a solution to mitigate this issue. The CTO also mentioned that CATL is working on a new battery that can be fully charged in 7 minutes, competing with BYD's similar technology.
