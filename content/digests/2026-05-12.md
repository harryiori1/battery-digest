## 01 UK Delivers Europe’s Largest Vanadium Flow Battery System
Invinity Energy Systems has delivered 20.7 megawatt-hours of vanadium flow batteries to the Copwood VFB Energy Hub in East Sussex. This project is set to be Europe’s largest vanadium flow battery installation once it enters service later in 2026. The Copwood VFB Energy Hub will provide energy storage for the UK grid, helping to stabilize the network and enable greater integration of renewable energy sources. Vanadium flow batteries are a type of long-duration energy storage technology that can store energy for several hours, making them well-suited for applications such as grid stabilization and peak shaving. ## 02 Ford Launches Ford Energy Subsidiary to Build 20 GWh of Battery Storage Annually
Ford has officially unveiled Ford Energy, a wholly owned subsidiary that will manufacture and sell U.S.-assembled battery energy storage systems (BESS) for utilities, data centers, and large industrial customers. The new unit plans to produce 20 GWh of energy storage annually from its Kentucky gigafactory. This move marks a significant expansion of Ford’s energy storage business, which is expected to play a key role in the company’s transition to electric vehicles. Ford Energy’s battery containers are designed to last 20 years and pack over 5 megawatt-hours of energy. [Electrek](https://electrek.co/2026/05/11/ford-energy-launches-battery-storage-subsidiary-20gwh-bess/)

## 03 SAIC Opens Pre-Orders for MG 4X Electric SUV with Semi-Solid-State Battery
SAIC has opened pre-orders in China for the new MG 4X, an electric SUV with a semi-solid-state EV battery, priced under $15,000. The new battery technology is set to launch in Europe by the end of 2026. The MG 4X features a range of up to 250 miles on a single charge, making it an attractive option for budget-conscious buyers. Semi-solid-state batteries offer improved safety, energy density, and charging performance compared to traditional lithium-ion batteries.
