## How We Work

Battery Digest curates the most important battery technology news from professional sources daily, distilling them into 3 in-depth stories and quick news items so you can stay informed in 5 minutes.

## Active Sources

We currently monitor the following sources:

**English**
- [Electrek](https://electrek.co/) — EV and clean energy news (RSS)
- [CnEVPost](https://cnevpost.com/) — China EV and battery news in English
- [InsideEVs](https://insideevs.com/) — Electric vehicle news and reviews (RSS)
- [CleanTechnica](https://cleantechnica.com/) — Clean energy and EV coverage (RSS)

**Chinese**
- [36Kr](https://36kr.com/) — Technology and business news (RSS)

## Scoring Criteria

Every incoming article is automatically scored across several dimensions:

1. **Cross-source corroboration**: When multiple independent sources cover the same story, it gets a score boost. This is the strongest importance signal — if only one source mentions it, it might only be important to that source
2. **Community engagement**: Upvotes and comment counts. Community consensus is another form of validation
3. **Source authority**: First-party company blogs (e.g., CATL announcing its own product) and top-tier tech outlets carry more weight than aggregator newsletters
4. **Recency**: Items from the last 24 hours get a boost, preventing stale stories from taking up space

Items scoring above a threshold enter the "highlight" pool; the rest go into the "notable" pool. All items are then considered in the editorial stage.

## Coverage Areas

- Power batteries (lithium-ion, LFP, NMC, etc.)
- Energy storage (grid-scale, commercial & industrial, residential)
- Solid-state batteries (sulfide, oxide, polymer)
- Sodium-ion batteries
- Emerging technologies (lithium-sulfur, lithium-air, flow batteries, etc.)
- Battery materials and recycling
- Industry policy and regulations

## Limitations

- We do not provide investment advice
- Coverage focuses on technology and industry, not stock or financial analysis
- Some sources may have timing delays
- AI-assisted content is reviewed by humans but may still contain biases
